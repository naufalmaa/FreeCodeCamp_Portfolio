Nelson Mandela

The man who stand against apartheid!

Nelson Mandela raised his hands a form of resistance against apartheid with his supporters.

This is timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918: Born on July 18 in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1941: Moves to Johannesburg and becomes involved in anti-colonial and African nationalist politics.
  • 1944: Co-founds the African National Congress (ANC) Youth League.
  • 1948: Apartheid officially instituted by the National Party in South Africa.
  • 1952: Launches the Defiance Campaign against apartheid laws and is elected as the ANC's National Volunteer-in-Chief.
  • 1956: Arrested and charged with treason along with 155 others; charges dropped in 1961.
  • 1960: The Sharpeville Massacre occurs, leading to the banning of the ANC.
  • 1961: Co-founds the armed wing of the ANC, Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK), and begins sabotage campaigns.
  • 1962: Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for leaving the country illegally and inciting workers to strike.
  • 1964: Sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage in the Rivonia Trial.
  • 1964-1990: Imprisoned on Robben Island, Pollsmoor Prison, and Victor Verster Prison.
  • 1990: Released from prison on February 11; becomes President of the ANC.
  • 1991: Elected President of the ANC at the organization's first national conference in South Africa since being banned in 1960.
  • 1993: Wins the Nobel Peace Prize alongside President F.W. de Klerk for their efforts to end apartheid.
  • 1994: Elected as South Africa's first black president in the country's first fully representative democratic election.
  • 1995: Establishes the Truth and Reconciliation Commission to investigate human rights abuses during the apartheid era.
  • 1999: Steps down as President after one term and focuses on charitable work through the Nelson Mandela Foundation.
  • 2001: Diagnosed with prostate cancer and begins treatment.
  • 2004: Retires from public life but continues to speak out on important issues.
  • 2009: Mandela Day is declared on his birthday, July 18, to promote global peace and celebrate his legacy.
  • 2013: Passes away on December 5 at the age of 95, leaving a profound legacy of peace, justice, and reconciliation.
